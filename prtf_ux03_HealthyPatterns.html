Healthy Patterns


Dedicated App & Mobile Website


The product

Healthy Patterns is located in a metropolitan area. As part of their involved in community activities, they want to help people learn how to cook healthier meals and are creating a new mobile application and responsive website to help customers find recipes and information on foods that offer maximum nutritional benefits. Patrons can save their favorite recipes and build their grocery lists and access them at their own discretion.


My role in the project


    Plan and conduct user research and competitor analysis.

    Create user stories and personas.

    User Flow and usability testing.

    Create paper and digital wireframes.

    Visual Design, accounting for accessibility and iterating on designs.

    Low and high fidelity prototyping.


Duration

1 month


Project goal

Ideate and design both: a dedicated app and mobile website for Healthy Patterns that will improve healthy nutrition for its clients by facilitating recipe organization, meal planning and grocery shopping.


Target audience

One of the simplest ways to improve your health is by preparing home-cooked meals. Busy workers and commuters lack the time necessary to search for healthy recipes, decide what groceries to buy and prepare new meals that might not meet expectations.


Empathize

Research study details

To understand our users and the challenges they might face in the meal planning, recipe searching, and grocery shopping process on the new Healthy Patterns' app and website a moderated usability study was conducted with 5 participants that didn't have time to learn how to prepare healthy meals.
Research helped gain a deeper understanding of the expectations users had when deciding to visit a website or use the app where they could find healthy, well rated recipes.


Key challenges or constraints

Time and convenience
Working adults are too busy to spend time searching for healthy recipes, decide what groceries to buy and prepare new meals that might not meet expectations.

Expectation
When deciding on a healthy recipe online the user should be able to gather some feedback and easily build a grocery list in an straightforward process.

Accessibility
Most platforms for healthy meal preparation offer poor accessibility features.

User Journey

Mapping user journey revealed how helpful and convenient would be for users to have access to a dedicated app and mobile website that will improve healthy nutrition by facilitating recipe organization, meal planning and grocery list creation.

Application: Test

User testing results

In order to develop mockups, two rounds of usability studies were conducted. Findings on quantitative and qualitative data helped guide the designs creation and subsequent revisions.

Round 1 findings

  • Most users would benefit by revising the lower navigation design.
  • Participants needed more information on what the term "Super Food" means.
  • When adding items to grocery list, it is preferable to pre-select items and allow them to deselect them.


Round 2 findings

  • Units and measurements can be useful when making an organized grocery list.

Key Takeaways

Conclusion

Impact:
The design for Healthy Patterns' app and website include features for users that were not taken into consideration by our direct competitors. The UX is consistent and perfectly aligned with the design's purpose of offering a pleasant and easy way for customers to search and save recipes, resulting in an intuitive and user-friendly experience for our target users, who shared that the design was intuitive to navigate through, and demonstrated a clear visual hierarchy.

What I learned:
Innovation is simply not a linear process. In order to reach a solution, the designs have to undergo an iterative process where designers continually improve the product. Each usability study provides helpful insights into what needs to be revised or updated.

Next Steps

Next steps:

  • There are some features that need to be tested again in order to provide a truthful evaluation. Additional rounds of testing could show if features like rating and comments can really influence product popularity.
  • In order to make the website more functional features could be added, for example viodeos of recipes. Each addition would need further testing before deploying.
